The reality

The world runs on ads—but accessibility is often ignored.

Companies rely on people to click through and purchase from ads. And the people they're relying on, of course, includes people with disabilities — but accessibility is often an afterthought. Blind and low-vision users especially face significant challenges with online ads, often making it difficult to understand what's being advertised or to complete a purchase.

The impact

Spending power of people with disabilities.

Disposable income of people with disabilities accounts for about $2.6 trillion. The disability market stretches beyond disabled people and includes friends and family—making the grand total global disability market worth about $18.3 trillion.

The work

Accessibility research and specifications.

We conducted research studies to understand barriers to online advertisements. We learned about users' experiences and preferences when navigating Yahoo sites and apps.

Overall findings indicated online ads are largely inaccessible and inconsistently implemented for screen reader users. While isolated examples of accessible ads exist, most ads lack proper accessibility. But, we can change that. There are some practical things we can all do right now.

Some practical things we can do right now

Place ads in clearly defined frames
Clearly label links and buttons including destination
Use concise and meaningful alt-text
Avoid auto-playing videos even if muted
Make ads easy to skip and don't steal focus
Offer brief context over hiding ads
